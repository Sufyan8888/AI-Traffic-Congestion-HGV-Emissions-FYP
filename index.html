⚠️ Data Leakage — Identified & Corrected

The initial model configuration included vehicle class component counts (cars, LGVs, buses) as predictor features alongside their aggregate total as the target variable. This introduced implicit data leakage producing an artificial R²=0.9999. The corrected model uses only 4 genuinely independent features: year, HGV%, road category and region. This correction is itself a methodological contribution — leakage of this type is rarely documented explicitly in traffic forecasting literature.

Spatial findings

What the maps reveal

M6 Corridor — Highest Intensity

The M6 motorway running through Lancashire and Cumbria into Scotland is the single most intense HGV corridor visible in both maps — deep red throughout its entire length.

M1/A1 Spine — East Midlands

The north-south M1 and A1 corridors through Yorkshire and East Midlands show consistently high HGV intensity, confirming the regional findings in the tabular analysis.

London Central — Visibly Lighter

Central London shows noticeably lower HGV intensity surrounded by higher-intensity orbital routes — spatial proof of ULEZ and congestion charge effectiveness.

Wales & South West — Sparse

Rural Wales and the South West peninsula show the lowest freight intensity nationally — consistent with distance from major logistics hubs and industrial centres.

Scotland Central Belt

The Glasgow-Edinburgh corridor shows a concentrated cluster of high-intensity points, confirming Scotland's highest national HGV percentage in the aggregate data.

The M25 orbital and radial routes around London show high HGV intensity, explaining why South East England records the highest cumulative HGV CO₂ emissions nationally.

Methodology

Six-stage research pipeline

Quantitative secondary data analysis from acquisition through ML model evaluation.

1

Data Acquisition — UK DfT AADF

Downloaded 555,880 records × 34 variables. 40,625 road count points, 11 regions, 2000–2023. Zero missing values confirmed.

2

Exploratory Data Analysis

16 visualisations covering traffic trends, vehicle composition, COVID impact, regional variation and urban/rural comparison.

3

Emission Estimation — E = Σ AADF × EF × link_length

DEFRA factors: Cars 180g/km · LGVs 220g/km · HGVs 900g/km · Buses 800g/km. Results treated as approximate estimates.

4

Feature Engineering & Data Leakage Correction

Engineered HGV%. Label-encoded road category and region. Removed component vehicle counts to prevent leakage. 4 independent features retained.

5

ML Model Development — RF & XGBoost

80/20 train-test split (seed=42). Random Forest: 100 trees. XGBoost: lr=0.1, depth=6, 100 estimators. Identical feature sets for fair comparison.

6

Evaluation — MAE, RMSE, R²

XGBoost: R²=0.5815, MAE=8,063, RMSE=11,111. Random Forest: R²=0.5592, MAE=8,069, RMSE=13,456. XGBoost selected as best model.
